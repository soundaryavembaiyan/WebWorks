ABOUT Snake

Love on Snakes

Welcome to my website. Snake venom immobilises prey and aids in digestion by breaking down flesh. Snakes are elongated, limbless, carnivorous reptiles of the suborder Serpentes. Like all other squamates, snakes are ectothermic, amniote vertebrates covered in overlapping scales.

Snake special


Snakes can't bite food so have to swallow it whole. Snakes have flexible jaws which allow them to eat prey bigger than their head! Snakes are found on every continent of the world except Antarctica. Snakes have internal ears but not external ones...

Types:
1. Long-Nosed Vine Snake (Ahaetulla oxyrhyncha) Mildly Venomous
2. Graceful Small-Headed Sea Snake (Hydrophis gracilis) Highly Venomous
3. Common Bronzeback Tree Snake (Dendrelaphis tristis ) Non Venomous
4. Himalayan Wolf Snake (Lycodon mackinnoni ) Non Venomous
5. Sind Krait (Bungarus sindanus) Highly Venomous
6. Gumprecht's Pit Viper (Trimeresurus gumprechti ) Mildly Venomous
7. Pope’s Pitviper (Popeia popeiorum) Mildly Venomous
8. Eastern Cat Snake (Boiga gokool) Mildly Venomous
